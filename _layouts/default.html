Interdisciplinary
Artist/Engineer

I’m an artist and an engineer based in Berlin, exploring the space where music, technology, and design meet. With a background in chemical engineering, I approach creativity as a system—connecting sonic and visual elements into coherent, immersive experiences. My current focus lies in interdisciplinary problem-solving and bridging the worlds of art and technology. With sound in general as my main medium, I explore generative and interactive systems as well as music production and experimental performances.

About

I’m pursuing a master’s degree in Design and Computation at the Berlin University of the Arts (UdK) and the Technical University of Berlin (TU). My work spans music, sound design, and interactive installations, often blending acoustic instruments with electronic and generative systems. Holding a bachelor’s in chemical engineering, I bring a structured yet experimental approach to my projects. Alongside my practice as a musician, guitar teacher, and sound engineer, I’m driven by the challenge of creating works that connect across disciplines and invite audiences to experience sound and space in new ways.
